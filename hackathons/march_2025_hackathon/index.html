    March 2025: AI Plugin Challenge

    Build AI Solutions. Solve Real Problems.

    March 15-17, 2025 | Toronto, ON

    Registration Status

    The Challenge

    Build an AI-powered plugin for Shopify that solves a real e-commerce problem for one of our partner businesses.

    Possible Projects

    Teams will choose from:

    1. Intelligent Product Recommendations
    2. Use ML to suggest products based on browsing behavior
    3. Integrate with Shopify's product catalog

    4. Measure conversion rate improvements

    5. AI-Powered Customer Support

    6. Build a chatbot that handles common customer queries
    7. Integrate with order history and product information

    8. Escalate complex issues to human support

    9. Inventory Forecasting Tool

    10. Predict inventory needs based on historical data
    11. Alert merchants about potential stockouts
    12. Optimize reorder timing

    Prerequisites

    Required Skills

    • Basic programming knowledge (Python or JavaScript)
    • Familiarity with APIs (REST/GraphQL)
    • Git/GitHub fundamentals
    • Understanding of web development concepts
    • Willingness to learn and collaborate!

    Partner Businesses

    We're working with 3 small e-commerce businesses in the Toronto area:

    GreenLeaf Organics

    Industry: Natural wellness products
    Challenge: Need intelligent product recommendations to increase average order value
    Data Available: 2 years of transaction history, 500+ products

    TechGear Plus

    Industry: Consumer electronics accessories
    Challenge: High volume of repetitive customer support queries
    Data Available: Customer support ticket history, product documentation

    ArtisanCraft Co

    Industry: Handmade home decor
    Challenge: Difficulty predicting seasonal inventory needs
    Data Available: 18 months sales data, inventory records, seasonal trends

    Each business owner will be available during the hackathon to answer questions and provide feedback.

    Judging Criteria

    Projects will be evaluated on:

    1. Business Impact (30%) - Does it solve a real problem? Is the ROI clear?
    2. Technical Implementation (30%) - Code quality, architecture, use of AI/ML
    3. Innovation (20%) - Creative approach to the problem
    4. Presentation (10%) - Clear communication of solution and value
    5. Deployment Readiness (10%) - How close to production-ready?

    FAQ

    Can I participate remotely?
    This hackathon is in-person only to facilitate better collaboration and mentorship.

    Do I need a team?
    No! We'll help form teams on Friday evening based on skills and interests. Solo registration is encouraged.

    What if I'm a beginner?
    Perfect! We structure teams with mixed experience levels and provide extensive mentorship. We've had complete beginners contribute meaningfully.

    Will the code I write be open source?
    Yes, with appropriate licensing. You retain rights to showcase your work in your portfolio.

    Is there food provided?
    Yes! All meals are provided: - Friday: Dinner - Saturday: Breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks - Sunday: Breakfast, lunch

    We accommodate dietary restrictions - please mention them during registration.

    Can I stay overnight at the venue?
    Yes, the venue is available 24/7. Bring a sleeping bag if you plan to stay overnight.

    What if we don't finish?
    We scope projects realistically, but partial solutions are absolutely fine. The goal is learning and building, not perfection.

    Will there be recruiting opportunities?
    Yes! Several of our partner companies attend to meet talented developers. Past participants have received job offers.
